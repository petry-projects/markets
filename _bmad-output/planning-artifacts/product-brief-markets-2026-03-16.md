# Product Brief: markets

<!-- Content will be appended sequentially through collaborative workflow steps -->

## Executive Summary

markets addresses a high-friction operational problem in local farmer and art markets: customers struggle to find accurate, current market-day information because managers must manually collect updates from many vendors through text messages, emails, and phone calls. This fragmented process leads to stale or inconsistent customer-facing information, causing missed purchases, reduced vendor sales, and lower overall market performance.

The product vision is to create a unified, real-time coordination layer that helps managers gather and publish trusted updates efficiently, helps vendors communicate availability with minimal effort, and helps customers make confident decisions before and during market day. The outcome is a more efficient market ecosystem with increased vendor revenue, stronger customer loyalty, and better manager execution.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Customers cannot reliably find accurate, current information about farmer and art markets because market managers struggle to collect and distribute real-time vendor details across a fragmented communication workflow.

### Problem Impact

The current system creates direct business and trust damage:
- Customers arrive and cannot find desired products.
- Customers encounter incorrect market hours or location details.
- Vendors lose sales opportunities and repeat buyers.
- Managers operate with low efficiency, reducing market quality and long-term success.

If unresolved, this drives continued inefficiency, weaker vendor profitability, weaker manager outcomes, and declining customer confidence in market participation.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Current practices depend on decentralized communication and broadcasting:
- Managers collect updates manually through text, email, and calls.
- Managers or vendors post updates independently on Instagram and Facebook.

These channels are not designed as a coordinated operational system. They are fragmented, inconsistent, and hard to verify in real time, which prevents reliable market-wide visibility for all participants.

### Proposed Solution

Build a market operations platform that centralizes market-day information flow across managers, vendors, and customers:
- Vendors provide quick, structured status and product updates.
- Managers view, validate, and publish consolidated market truth efficiently.
- Customers receive clear, current, market-level and vendor-level information before and during market visits.

The product should optimize for low update friction, high information freshness, and high customer confidence so that markets operate more efficiently and vendors sell more.

### Key Differentiators

- Domain-native design: built specifically for farmer and art market workflows rather than generic social posting behavior.
- Multi-actor coordination: one operational source of truth for customer, vendor, and manager needs.
- Efficiency-to-revenue focus: designed to increase vendor sales and return customers through better information quality.
- Founder advantage: custom software delivery at a fraction of traditional cost, combining product expertise, market context, and agentic development capabilities.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**1) Rachel - Health-Conscious Weekly Customer (Urban Market Shopper)**

Rachel shops at urban weekly markets and wants fresh, local, organic produce for weekly cooking. She values convenience, confidence, and speed when deciding where to shop.

Current pain:
- She cannot reliably tell which vendors are present in real time.
- She may arrive and find desired products unavailable.
- She may lose trust when hours/location details are inaccurate.

Current workaround:
- Checks Instagram/Facebook posts from markets and individual vendors.
- Uses fragmented updates and best guesses before traveling.

Success vision:
- Rachel can quickly search and find her favorite vendors and products.
- She can trust current hours, location, and availability.
- She feels empowered and informed before leaving home.

**2) Brad - Multi-Market Organic Produce Vendor**

Brad is a local organic farmer selling at farmers markets five days per week. His primary goal is to sell out daily and increase repeat buyers.

Current pain:
- Customers miss him because market-day information is inconsistent.
- Manual communication with managers is repetitive and inefficient.
- Missed customer demand leads directly to lower same-day revenue.

Current workaround:
- Sends updates through text, email, or phone to managers.
- Posts on social media independently, with no unified market channel.

Success vision:
- Customers find Brad through the app and convert into purchases.
- He can publish updates quickly with low effort.
- Sales increase and repeat customer flow improves.

### Secondary Users

**Market Manager (Secondary but Critical Enabler)**

Market managers benefit from the solution through operational simplification and better attendance outcomes. They are not the first adopter in your model, but they are essential for scaling trust and coordination across all vendors.

Current pain:
- Information collection from vendors is manual and fragmented.
- Publishing timely, accurate market updates is labor-intensive.
- Operational inefficiency reduces market performance for all participants.

Success vision:
- They feel prepared and organized.
- More customers attend while manager workload decreases.
- They can run a higher-performing market with less coordination overhead.

### User Journey

**Customer Journey (Rachel):**
- Discovery: hears about the app through market community channels and peers.
- Onboarding: creates account, follows favorite vendors, sets product interests.
- Core usage: runs a quick search before market day to confirm vendor presence, products, and logistics.
- Aha moment: "I easily found my favorite vendors and food with a quick search."
- Long-term behavior: uses the app weekly as a trusted planning tool and returns more consistently.

**Vendor Journey (Brad):**
- Discovery: sees growing customer usage and demand from customers to use the app.
- Onboarding: creates vendor profile, links products, sets market schedule.
- Core usage: posts fast market-day status and availability updates with minimal effort.
- Aha moment: "People find me through the app and come make a purchase."
- Long-term behavior: uses app as a daily revenue channel and repeat-customer engine.

**Manager Journey (Secondary):**
- Discovery: notices improved customer and vendor engagement around app-enabled markets.
- Onboarding: configures market profile, hours, and vendor roster.
- Core usage: validates and publishes consolidated market updates with less manual chasing.
- Aha moment: "More people are coming even though my job has become easier."
- Long-term behavior: relies on the platform as the operational source of truth.

## Success Metrics

Success for markets is measured by whether customers can discover relevant vendors faster, vendors gain meaningful visibility that converts into sales, and market managers can maintain accurate, complete market-day information with less effort.

### User Success Metrics (First 90 Days)

- Customer success:
  - Discovery of markets and vendors increases by 100 follows.
  - Customers can identify relevant market/vendor options with fewer failed trips.
- Vendor success:
  - Vendor follows increase by 10 per participating vendor.
  - Increased visibility leads to stronger purchase intent from app-discovering customers.
- Manager success:
  - Accuracy and completeness of vendor information increases by 10 vendors with complete/current profiles.

### Business Objectives

- 3-month objectives:
  - 5 markets onboarded
  - 5 market/vendor managers onboarded
  - 40 vendors onboarded
  - 250 customers onboarded
- 12-month objectives:
  - 20 markets onboarded
  - 20 market/vendor managers onboarded
  - 160 vendors onboarded
  - 1000 customers onboarded

### Key Performance Indicators

- Monthly active customers: 100
- Vendor participation rate: 40
- Weekly returning customer rate: 10%
- Active market managers: 10

### Operational Health and Guardrail Metrics

- Onboarding drop-off rate (customer and vendor funnels)
- Vendor update frequency (market-day status/availability update cadence)
- Inaccurate listing complaint volume and trend

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

- Market and manager onboarding
- Vendor onboarding
- Customer signup
- Search for markets and vendors
- Follow markets and vendors

These core features form the minimum viable loop:
1. Supply side can join (markets/managers + vendors).
2. Demand side can join (customers).
3. Customers can discover and subscribe to market/vendor updates.

### Out of Scope for MVP

- In-app chat
- Ratings and reviews
- Weather functionality
- Inventory management
- Market fee management

These are intentionally deferred to protect launch speed and keep the MVP centered on discovery and coordination.

### MVP Success Criteria

MVP is considered successful when markets, managers, vendors, and customers are all onboarded and actively interacting through the core flows:
- onboarding completion
- search usage
- follow actions between customers and markets/vendors

Validation signal:
- the ecosystem is not only signed up, but demonstrating ongoing cross-role interaction.

### Future Vision

- Expand from initial launch footprint to a multi-city and multi-state rollout.
- Evolve from local pilot operations into a regional network of connected markets, vendors, and customers.
